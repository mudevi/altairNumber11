My Altair Design Story

When I was around six, on one of our family trips to the Science Centre, my Mom bought me a mind-altering coloring book. It was a set of geometric patterns that were co-created by Ensor Holiday and Roger Burrows, a biologist and a writer/designer. The patterns are based on a form of Islamic design that features closely packed circles of varying sizes. They are called Altair designs and were published in a series of four coloring books through the seventies.

Eventually, my book was packed away with all the other stuff I had outgrown, and by the time I had my first nostalgic memory of it, it was long gone. Then, a couple of years ago, I stepped into a random vintage store on a break from work and there was More Altair Designs, first edition, but in far better shape than I had last seen my original copy.

The designs above were derived from a JPEG image of an Altair Design #11 page. The image was converted to vectors in Illustrator, but needed a lot of hand-done adjustments before being used as inline SVG. As a result, the patterns are mathematically imperfect and you will probably notice little anomalies here and there if you look carefully.
